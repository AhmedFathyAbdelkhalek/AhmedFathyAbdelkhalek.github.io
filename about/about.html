Ahmed Fathy Abdelkhalek

I am a proactive and diligent BSc Mechatronics Engineering graduate from the German University in Cairo with experience in robotics, control, and R&D. I possess strong mathematical, analytical, and creative thinking skills with fluency in Arabic and English and can work independently. I am keen on pursuing a career in mechatronics engineering with a focus on designing, developing, and enhancing robotic and mechatronic systems.

I did my bachelor thesis at the German University in Cairo under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Ayman A. El-Badawy. The thesis models the kinematics and dynamics of a novel 3-DoF Delta parallel robot and applies both linear and nonlinear control laws for trajectory tracking. This is in order to control the movement of nano-carriers in drug delivery applications. The study was published at the IEEE Novel Intelligent and Leading Emerging Sciences Conference (NILES) and received the 3rd Best Paper Award. You can read it here.

I am currently pursuing a master's degree at the German University in Cairo in mechatronics engineering with a heavy focus on robotics to develop a robotic gripper for harvesting horticultural produce. Additionally, I am a teaching assistant in the Engineering Design and Production Department at the GUC, aiding in the development and instruction of several courses that highlight different design and manufacturing tools and techniques.

On a more personal level, I play table tennis at a local club, am an avid gamer, and did professional music production a few years ago. If you would like to connect with or contact me, please feel free to reach out.
